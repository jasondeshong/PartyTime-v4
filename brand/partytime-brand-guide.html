01
Brand Essence

Everyone's digital jukebox.

PartyTime puts the music in everyone's hands. No coins, no gatekeepers — just the room deciding what plays next. Old-soul music culture meets frictionless modern tech.

Warm, not corporate

The product feels like a warm lamp in a dark room. Inviting, ambient, human.

Transparent, not opaque

Like a Nothing Phone or clear-shell Walkman — the beauty is in the visible structure. Show the grid. Expose the skeleton.

Chic, not geek

Technology that wears well. The interface is fashion-forward, not dashboard-heavy. Every pixel is considered.

Core Tension

The warmth of a jukebox in a dive bar, the precision of an Apple product, the honesty of transparent hardware. These three things shouldn't work together — but they do.

What We're Not

Not a DJ app. Not a social network. Not themed. Not dark-and-edgy. We're warm-and-inviting with visible bones.

04
Texture & Surface

Transparent by design.

Inspired by clear-shell hardware — the Nothing Phone, transparent Walkmans, iMac G3. The UI shows its structure. Glass surfaces, visible grids, dot-matrix patterns.

Glass Surface
Exposed Grid
Dot Matrix
Scan Lines
  • 01 Glass is the primary surface. Cards use translucent backgrounds (4-7% Papyrus) with subtle borders. Like frosted acrylic over a dark chassis.
  • 02 Grid reveals structure. Subtle grid overlays can appear behind hero sections — like the visible PCB traces on a Nothing Phone. Never over text.
  • 03 Dot-matrix for accents. Loading states, empty-state backgrounds, or subtle pattern fills. References Artifact's branding and LED displays on vintage audio.
  • 04 Scan lines are a whisper. 2px repeating lines at 2% opacity. Only on transitions or background textures. If you have to squint to see them, they're right.

Iconography & Symbols

Hidden in plain sight.

Egyptian symbols woven subtly into the geometry of the product. Never literal, never themed. A first-time user should never think "Egyptian." A returning user might notice and smile.

The Rule

Abstract, never illustrative. Use the proportions and geometry of Egyptian symbols, not their visual likeness. These become part of the UI structure — not decorations layered on top. Discoverable, not obvious.

06
Logo System

Mark & wordmark.

PARTYTIME

Wordmark

Inter 800, tight tracking. All caps. Clean and direct. The wordmark is the primary identifier in-app and on marketing materials.

Icon Mark

Sistrum abstracted into a modern mark. The U-shaped frame of the ancient instrument becomes a rounded arch, with a play triangle nested where the crossbars would be. Reads as "play" to everyone, "sistrum" to those who know. Hathor's instrument — goddess of music and joy.

07
Motion

Physics, not timelines.

Spring-based, never linear. Quick and warm. Every animation communicates a state change — nothing moves for its own sake.

08
Brand Voice

Casual but considered.

Write like you're texting a friend who has good taste. Confident, minimal, human. Say it once.

Do Add a song to get the party started
Don't No songs in queue. Please add a song to begin playback.
Do Couldn't connect — is Spotify open?
Don't Error: Connection to Spotify failed. Please try again.
Do queued by Jay
Don't Added by: Jay DeShong
Do Saved to library
Don't Success! Song has been saved to your Spotify library.

Words we use

Queue. Lobby. Host. Skip. Vibe (sparingly).

Words we avoid

Error. Failure. Invalid. Please. "Oops." Exclamation marks in UI (toasts only).

Application

In context.

Glass surfaces, warm glow, visible structure. The album art is the hero — the UI frames it, never competes. Controls are confident and minimal.

  • 01 Now Playing is the hero. Largest card, amber glow shadow, album rolodex, full track info + controls.
  • 02 Queue items are glass cards. Translucent, bordered, with vote controls and queue position.
  • 03 Search is utility. Compact rows, minimal chrome. Don't decorate — get out of the way.
